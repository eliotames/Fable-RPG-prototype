# Prototype Scope

- The prototype scope is a single, self-contained combat encounter playtesting environment.
- Keep every disparate combat system mechanic or interaction decoupled from progression-related mechanics and systems like skills and attributes. Occasionally the player is hit with passive consequences of an especially low attribute or skill. In this case, decouple the consequence from the attribute or skill.

Derived statistics need to have their mechanical functions defined, but attributes (which bridge between combat and all other game systems) are external inputs to the combat system, not part of the combat system itself, and should be left entirely undefined, with combat-dependent stats tuned directly rather than through derived formulas.

*Reasoning: once the prototype is fully playable, all these elements can be indexed to establish what should be grouped as skills, attributes, class-specific mechanics, etc.*

# Combat Framework

## Foundational Overview

- Combat makes use of a deep and interconnected system of AP economy, basic attacks and abilities, items, enemy action telegraphing, damage types and resistances, status conditions, and environmental conditions.
- The player controls a **party of four heroes**. The party must have at least one each of: warrior, rogue, and mage.

## Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Hero** | Any active player-controlled party member. |
| **Enemy** | Any active computer-controlled combat unit. |
| **Combatant** | Any active combat unit on either side. |
| **Turn** | A combatant's turn, during which they perform actions. |
| **Turn queue** | The full turn-order queue of all heroes and enemies in an encounter. |
| **Basic attack** | A simple attack using the combatant's weapon or innate magic. |
| **Ability** | A special weapon-based or spell-based action. |
| **Incapacitated** | A hero whose HP has reached 0 and has entered the revival window but has not yet died. |
| **Eliminated** | A combatant who has died and been removed from the encounter/run. |
| **Guard** | A combat resource generated by abilities that negates incoming damage entirely until depleted. |

## Temporal Model

- Combat is **turn-based**. There are no real-time mechanics.
- Entities act one at a time in sequence.
- Sequence is stat-derived, and initiative is based on stat-modified rolls.
- Turn structure and AP economy are the primary per-encounter pacing mechanisms.
- Aggressive enemy pressure discourages stalling; legible enemy intent enables confident, fast play.
- Game systems, ability design, and enemy design all actively modify or disrupt the per-encounter AP economy rhythm.

## Action Economy and Pacing

- AP is the single in-combat resource gate. **Cooldowns are excluded by design** — pacing is
driven by AP costs and enemy pressure, not timers.
- On each turn a hero may use up to **one Major Action** and **one Minor Action**. Both generally cost AP, scaled to effect and potency.
- **Major Actions** include basic attacks, abilities, and the use of advanced or powerful items.
- **Minor Actions** include repositioning and formation control, auxiliary actions, and the use of basic items.

### Stamina (AP)

- Combatants begin at **3 AP**.
- At the start of each combatant's turn, they generate **1 AP** by default.
- Unspent AP carries over between turns.
- A basic attack is a standard Major Action that generates 1 AP rather than consuming AP.

### Enemy Economy

- Enemy behavior is **engineered AI/tactics, not governed by AP economy**.

### Turn Order

- Each combatant has an **action timer** valued **0–100** (never below 0).
- At the start of each combatant's turn, **every** combatant's timer decrements by their **Speed**.
- When a combatant ends their turn, their timer **resets to 100**.
- The combatant with the **lowest** timer after a turn ends goes next. Ties at 0 → **highest Speed** goes next.

## Spatial Model

- Each combat encounter takes place in an individual battle scene, with varying enemy composition and environmental elements.
- Combat uses tactical battlefield formations and positioning.
- The battlefield consists of the player side and the enemy side. Each side has two lanes: **Frontline** and **Backline**.
- The two sides form top and bottom rows in an interlocking hex grid of point-up-hexagons, with two rows of no-mans-land between.
- Each lane has multiple **spaces**, occupied or empty. Combatants are **not fixed to a lane** — as an action they may move into an empty space or **swap places** with an ally.
- A combatant's available targets depend on **battlefield position** and **targeting angles**, the mechanics of which can differ based on the attack or ability used.
- **Melee** attacks and abilities can be used **only from the Frontline**, and can only target combatants in the opposing Frontline.
- **Ranged** attacks and abilities can be used from either lane, but suffer an accuracy penalty when used from the Frontline.
- Positioning, lane and angle exploitation, and reading enemy intentions are central to the player's tactical decision-making.

### Obstacles and Hazards

- In order to use a melee attack, a combatant must cross no-mans land.
- There are two types of obstacles:
    - Object: can't be landed on but can be traveled between.
    - Line: can't be landed on, crossed, or traveled between.
- Different cover grants different effects (full cover = large accuracy penalty; half cover = moderate penalty; incomplete cover like a fence = small penalty)
- Obstacles can exist both in no man's land and within a given side (affects formation and positioning rather than just attack availability).
- Allows for cool battlefield layouts/environmental set-ups, like a river with a single bridge and both teams on either side of the river.
- Allows for hazards.

Types of obstacles:
- Tree (object)
- Fence (line)
- Stream/River (line)
- Crevice (line)
- Crates and Barrels (objects)

## Core Stats and Resources

**Stamina**:
- Per-encounter action points. The primary in-combat action gate.

**Health**:
- Physical health. Lost by taking damage.
- If a hero reaches 0 HP, they are incapacitated and enter the revival window.
- If most enemies reach 0 HP, they are immediately eliminated.
- If some special enemies reach 0 HP, they are incapacitated.

**Willpower**:
- Chance to avoid mental attacks.
- Willingness to continue while hurt or exhausted (saving throw, sanity, mental resistance).
- Depletable: exhausts with use and fatigue, replenishes with rest and recovery (reduced by losing saving throws and taking mental damage).

**Composure**:
- Regulation under pressure (accuracy, fear/panic resistence).
- Depletable: degrades under sustained stress, fatigue, or emotional overload.

**Power** (attack and ability modifier):
- Determined by weapon and scaling.

**Accuracy**:
- Derived from **Coordination**, **Composure**, and **Weapon** (specific trained domain: class + weapon skill).

**Speed**:
- Initiative and turn frequency.
- Derived from **Agility** and **Mobility**.

**Melee Evasion**:
- Chance to avoid melee attacks.
- Derived from **Coordination** and weapon/equipment.

**Ranged Evasion**:
- Chance to avoid ranged and area-of-effect attacks.
- Derived from **Agility** and weapon/equipment.

**Resistance**:
- Damage reduction per damage type.
- Derived from **Constitution** and outfit/armor

**Guard**:
- How much Guard a combatant produces from abilities.

### Attributes

- Strength
- Constitution
- Agility
- Coordination
- Attunement: capacity to perceive the supernatural (detect hidden spirits, see through illusions).

## Core Systems

Weapons and Equipment:
- Attack power multiplier
- Basic attack form
- Accuracy modifier
- Defense modifier
- 1 item-specific ability (small pool, overlapping item abilities)

Outfits and Armor:
- Base resistances
- Mobility

Perks and Potions:
- Grant temporary/permanent buffs and/or debuffs (such as increasing resolution mechanics, checks, or resistences, etc.).

Talents:
- Grant unique access to certain abilities or options.

Abilities:
- (Do not distinguish between abilities and spells. This distinction is class-based, not system-based.)
- Abilities can target enemies, allies, or the environment.
- *Unresolved design:* Whether healing abilities should be class-specific, gated by class, or even available as abilities at all.
